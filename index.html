About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Tufts University, advised by Dr. Elaine Short in the Assistive Agent and Behavior Learning lab (AABL). I work on improving robot learning from multi-modular human teaching, including human feedback, human demonstrations, and outputs from VLM/LLM. Most of my data is open-sourced and collected from public spaces with real-world non-expert users.

Actively open to research collaborations in HRI, RLHF for robots, and human data collection methodology.
